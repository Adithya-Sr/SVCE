SWALLOWTAILS- PAPILIONIDAE

COMMON MORMON

Papilio polytes

Habits

Meandering flight but unidirectional with moderate speed. Mud-puddles; Local migrant. Basks in the sun with its wings spread

Description

Large, jet black male with a white band running through the centre of the lower wing; has a prominent tail. Females mimic Common Rose and Crimson Rose, distinguished by its black body

COMMON ROSE

Pachliopta aristolochiae

Habits

Slow flier but straight and long-sustained, flies close to the ground, basks with wings spread out

Description

Large black butterfly with crimson body. White-streaked fore wings; hind wings tailed with white patches surrounded by red spots.

CRIMSON ROSE

Pachliopta hector

Habits

Slow, fluttering but steady and straight flight. Basks with wings spread flat. Stronger, faster than Common Rose. Migratory

Description

Large, glossy, black butterfly with two broad white bands on the fore wings (which is absent in Common Rose); hind wings tailed with crimson crescents. Body is crimson red. Female paler in colouration.

LIME BUTTERFLY

Papilio demoleus

Habits

Rapid fliers, mud-puddles, active nectar feeder. Basks with spread wings

Description

Black butterfly with irregular yellow spots. Hind wings has a prominent brick red spot with blue patches. Lacks a prominent tail. Underside is paler than upperside.
