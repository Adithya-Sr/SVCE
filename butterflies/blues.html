Blues-LYCAENIDAE

COMMON CERULEAN

Jamides celeno

Habits

Slow and erratic low flight, never basks with its wings spread out. Prefers partially-lit places

Description

Marked with long straight lines. The butterfly is tailed. Upperside is bluish white with a black border; underside is brown with white bands and a small eyespot located above the tail

DARK GRASS BLUE

Zizeeria karsandra

Habits

Weak flier, flies very close to the ground; often seen basking with its wings half open. Can be seen in great numbers in grassy habitats

Description

Upperside is dark blue, underside is usually brown, occasionally grey, spots are rounded and dark

FORGET-ME-NOT

Catochrysops strabo

Habits

Flight is fast, prefers open dry habitats, always in sunshine

Description

Pale grey underside with a small costal spot on the fore wing. Tailed hind wing, with two black spots along costa and has larger, yellow-crowned, tornal spot above tail on the underside. The male has shining violet-blue upperside and the female is pale brown with pale silvery blue

GRAM BLUE

Euchrysops cnejus

Habits

Fond of sunshine. Flies half a meter from the ground. Weak fliers, persistent and flutter for a long time

Description

Underside is pale buff with thin lines and spots; hind wing has four black dots near the base and two inwardly crowned orange and black tornal spots. Has a pair of string like tails

INDIAN SUNBEAM

Curetis thetis

Habits

Flight powerful, seen at lower elevations in woodlands. Often found before and after rains

Description

Tailless, glossy white underside lacking black dots. Males are golden red on the upperside while the females are white with a broad border

LARGE OAKBLUE

Arhopala amantes

Habits

Swift flight. Seen in moist patches and foliage

Description

Second largest Lycaenid to be found in Southern India. Upperside is radiant purplish blue with a thin black border. Underside is chocolate or pale greyish brown with brown speckles that are outlined with white

LIME BLUE

Chilades lajus

Habits

Modest, fluttering flight, close to shrubs and small trees, settles often. At rest or feeding wings are held erect and during basking may keep them half open

Description

Tailless. Brown underside with diagnostic ‘L’ shaped dots. Tornal spots are common for both sexes. But males have purplish-blue upperside and females have light blue at the basal area

ORIENTAL GRASS JEWEL

Freyeria putli

Habits

Commonly seen fluttering among low growing plants. Prefers open and dry habitats

Description

Upperside brown; underside grey to pale brown, spotted with dark brown. Tailless with a row of metallic orange crowned spots bordering underside of hind wings.

SMALL CUPID

Chilades parrhasius

Habits

Flies close to the ground. Prefers damp patches

Description

Light buff underside with two orange crowned tornal spots. Similar to Gram Blue, except it has three spots near the base on the hind wing. Upperside is purplish blue with a darker outer border

TINY GRASS BLUE

Zizula hylax

Habits

Weak flight, fluttering and in short bouts, settles frequently; flies close to the ground

Description

Tailless hind wing. Underside is pale greyish brown with small, distinct ‘L’ or ‘U’ shaped postdiscal spots on the fore wing, well separated and angled inwards. Top is dark brown fading to fawn towards the base

ZEBRA BLUE

Leptotes plinius

Habits

Quick but weak flier. Can fly for a long time. Males are active while females are not that active

Description

Wings are translucent, undersides have marbled brown and white zebra pattern which has probablly given it its name. Upperside of male is pale violet-blue and female is brown in colour
